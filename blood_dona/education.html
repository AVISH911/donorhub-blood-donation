Blood Donation Process

Learn what to expect when you donate blood

1

Registration

You'll complete a donor registration form with your personal information and medical history.

2

Health Screening

A quick health check including temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and hemoglobin level.

3

Donation

The actual donation takes about 8-10 minutes during which approximately one pint of blood is collected.

4

Refreshment

After donating, you'll enjoy refreshments to help your body adjust to the slight decrease in fluid.

After Donation Care

Important tips for your recovery after donating blood

Hydrate

Drink plenty of fluids over the next 24-48 hours to help your body replenish fluids.

Eat Well

Consume iron-rich foods to help your body rebuild its iron stores.

Avoid Strenuous Activity

Avoid heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the rest of the day.

Donor Eligibility

Basic requirements to become a blood donor

General Requirements

  • Be in good general health and feeling well
  • Be at least 17 years old in most states
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • Not have donated blood in the last 56 days
  • Have a valid ID with photo or signature

Temporary Restrictions

  • Cold or flu in the last 7 days
  • Pregnancy (wait 6 weeks after delivery)
  • Recent tattoo or piercing (wait 3-12 months)
  • Certain medications (check with staff)
  • Travel to malaria-risk areas (wait 3 months)

Permanent Restrictions

  • HIV/AIDS infection
  • Hepatitis B or C infection
  • Babesiosis or Chagas disease
  • Certain types of cancer
  • Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD)

Health Conditions & Donation

How specific health conditions affect your ability to donate

Heart & Lung Conditions

Most heart conditions don't prevent donation if well-controlled. Asthma is generally acceptable if you're not having an attack.

Medications

Most medications won't prevent donation. Blood thinners, Accutane, and some others may require a waiting period.

Surgery & Procedures

Wait until fully recovered from surgery. For minor procedures without complications, wait 24 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about blood donation

How long does the entire donation process take?

The entire process, from registration to post-donation refreshments, takes about one hour. The actual blood donation only takes 8-10 minutes.

Is donating blood safe?

Yes, donating blood is very safe. New, sterile disposable equipment is used for each donor, so there's no risk of contracting a bloodborne infection.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made more frequently - up to 24 times per year.

Does donating blood hurt?

You may feel a slight sting when the needle is inserted, but it only lasts a second. Most donors report little to no discomfort during donation.

How much blood is taken during donation?

For a whole blood donation, approximately one pint (about 470ml) is collected. Your body has about 10 pints of blood and replaces the donated fluid within 24 hours.

What should I do before donating?

Eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of fluids before your donation. Avoid fatty foods, as they can affect blood tests. Get a good night's sleep the night before.

Universal Donors & Recipients

Universal Donor

O- blood type is the universal donor because it can be given to patients of any blood type. This makes it especially valuable in emergency situations.

Universal Recipient

AB+ blood type is the universal recipient because people with this blood type can receive red blood cells from any blood type.

Myths & Facts About Blood Donation

Separating fact from fiction

Donating blood is painful

While you may feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, most donors report little to no pain during the donation process.

You can donate if you have diabetes

Yes, as long as your diabetes is well-controlled with medication or diet and you are otherwise healthy.

Donating blood will make you weak

Your body replaces the fluid lost during donation within 24 hours and red blood cells within 4-6 weeks. Most people feel fine after donating.

You need to know your blood type to donate

No, you don't need to know your blood type beforehand. Your blood will be typed after donation.

Vegetarians can't donate blood

Vegetarians can donate blood as long as they meet the hemoglobin requirements. Iron from plant sources is sufficient.

One donation can save up to three lives

Yes, blood can be separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets, each helping different patients.
